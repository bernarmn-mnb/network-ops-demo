# EE — Meeting Prep

## Customer
- **Company**: EE (part of BT Group)
- **Vertical**: Telecommunications / Mobile Network Operator
- **HQ**: London, UK
- **Size**: ~550 employees, 27M+ mobile customers

## Opportunity
Meeting with EE's Digital Customer Experience team in ~2 weeks. They're looking at
AI-powered customer support. Currently handling millions of support contacts per month
across phone, chat, and self-service channels.

## What I Know
- EE is the UK's largest mobile network operator
- They've been investing heavily in digital self-service (My EE app, online help centre)
- Pain points: high call centre volume, customers struggling to self-serve for
  common issues (billing, network troubleshooting, device setup)
- They've seen competitors (Vodafone, Three) experimenting with AI chatbots
- Their audience for this meeting is a mix: head of digital CX (business), plus
  a couple of platform engineers who'd need to evaluate the tech

## My Thinking
Not sure exactly what to build yet. I know they care about reducing support volume
and improving first-contact resolution. An AI support assistant feels obvious but I
want to make sure we show something that stands out from a basic chatbot.

Open to ideas on what would land best with this audience.
